Ph.D thesis - Three essays on digital platforms and their impact on labour

Sapienza University of Rome · defended May 2026

Essay I — Territories

When Amazon comes to town

What happens to a local labour market when an Amazon facility opens? A staggered difference-in-differences design on Italian municipal tax records outlines the effects on local employment and incomes.

MEF-IRPEF · local labour markets · Staggered DiD

Essay II — Firms

Platform-dependent firms

Many firms became dependent on digital platforms. Using Italian firm-level survey data, this essay links platform use with increased worker flexibility.

INAPP-DPS · firm-level analysis · matching, DiD

Essay III — Workers

Platform workers’ incomes

Are platform workers worse off than comparable workers in the traditional economy, and why? Probit models with coarsened exact matching on Italian labour force data.

INAPP-PLUS · worker-level · Probit, CEM

Who I am

I’m an applied economist based in Rome. I completed my PhD at Sapienza University of Rome under the supervision of Dario Guarascio and Valeria Cirillo, working at the intersection of labour economics, the economics of innovation and regional economics.

My research is concerned with the consequences for people, communities, and the planet of modern techno-economic power structures

Outside the office I’m engaged in grassroots activism in Rome, I like hiking and climbing in my home region of Abruzzo, and I’m always down to cook dinner (and eat, yum).
